Scenario
Q4 arrives. Marcus realises his $1,500 Delta Dental PPO benefit resets in January and he's used none of it. He commits to booking a cleaning and check-up before year-end.
He opens Google on his iPhone and searches "dentist near the Loop that takes Delta Dental PPO." He's done this before and been burned by outdated listings, so he's braced for friction.
He lands on Opencare. The insurance filter is his first move. He then compares 2–3 in-network practices by rating and distance, reading 5–10 reviews per dentist before shortlisting.
He submits a new patient request (NPR) at 9:30pm from his phone — outside business hours. He expects a quick response. He goes to his lunchtime appointment and the experience matches expectations.
Appointment done. He gets a receipt. He expects a reminder for his 6-month follow-up and ideally a one-click rebooking. He may refer a colleague if the app makes it effortless.

Emotions & Mindset
😐 Neutral → Motivated
"I've been putting this off, but I'm going to lose money if I don't act." Financially driven urgency, but no anxiety about the dental visit itself.
😤 Frustrated → Cautious
"I've been burned by out-of-date insurance info before. I'm not booking anywhere until I'm 100% sure they're in-network." Heightened skepticism at this stage.
🔍 Analytical → Focused
"OK, this looks promising. Let me verify the insurance, check the reviews, confirm the location." Task-oriented, comparing options methodically before committing.
⏳ Impatient → Relieved
"I submitted at night — when will they confirm?" Dip in confidence during the wait period. Strong relief when confirmation comes promptly. Post-visit: validated his research.
✅ Satisfied → Loyal
"Good dentist, zero surprise billing, experience matched the reviews." If the app sends a timely 6-month reminder, Marcus will rebook without any external trigger.

Inaccurate in-network listings on insurance portals and competitor directories erode trust pre-Opencare

54% of booking requests happen after hours — friction if confirmation is slow
Results page doesn't always clearly distinguish PPO vs HMO coverage
No upfront cost estimate for a standard cleaning at the selected practice
Post-NPR silence — patient app shows "pending" status with no ETA, creating uncertainty
Practice confirmation call may not come until next business morning
No proactive 6-month rebooking nudge — Marcus will wait for an external reminder
Review request buried or generic — low conversion rate

Scenario
A persistent dull ache that Priya has been mentioning to her roommate for months finally tips her into action. She knows she needs to go but fear has been the dominant force. The ache breaks through the avoidance.
She goes to Reddit (r/askTO) first — peer recommendations over ads. She finds Opencare recommended. She then spends 30–45 minutes reading reviews, looking at office photos, and checking Instagram before clicking through.
On Opencare, she reads the Funnel step "How do you feel about visiting?" and feels seen. She applies a "gentle / anxiety-friendly" filter. She reads 15–20 reviews looking for mentions of specific dentists' names and bedside manner.
She submits the NPR and immediately feels the post-submission silence as threatening. She checks the patient app repeatedly. Any delay amplifies anxiety. Once confirmed and she's met the dentist, the experience is transformative.
Post-appointment Priya is a different person — she's proud she went. If the experience was as gentle as promised, she becomes a loyal evangelist and will refer others. She needs a 6-month nudge that meets her emotional register.

Emotions & Mindset

🔦 Apprehensive → Searching
"Maybe if I find the right dentist it won't be so bad." Cautious optimism during research. She's processing slowly under stress — vague info makes anxiety worse.

🌟 Proud → Evangelistic
"I actually did it. And it was fine." Priya's post-visit state is unique — she's not just satisfied, she's proud of herself. If the platform nurtures this, she becomes a powerful word-of-mouth source.

Scenario
Tomás wakes up with a worsening toothache. He doesn't have a regular dentist. He's tried calling practices before and been put on hold or told the next available is in 3 weeks. He needs someone today or tomorrow.
At 10pm from his phone, he Googles "emergency dentist near me Houston open tomorrow." He finds several options. He opens Opencare — the "Dental Emergency" funnel path immediately addresses his situation and raises his confidence.
He moves through the emergency funnel rapidly. He has low tolerance for multi-step filtering. He needs to see availability immediately. The first credible result with "available this week" visible wins his booking.
He submits an NPR at 10pm. He falls asleep. His biggest risk: if confirmation doesn't come by 8am, he calls a different practice. Speed of response is the critical variable — the practice that responds first gets the booking.
Pain resolved. Tomás is grateful. But he will forget Opencare exists unless he's given a strong reason to return — specifically, a path to becoming a regular patient at this dentist now that trust is established.

Emotions & Mindset
😣 Pain → Desperation
"This hurts and I need to fix it now." Zero consideration phase — Tomás is not researching, he's triaging. The emotional state is acute discomfort driving impulse action.
⚡ Urgent → Scanning
"Please just show me someone available soon." Impatient with any friction. If the emergency path on Opencare is clear and fast, his confidence rises quickly. If it's slow, he bounces immediately.
🎯 Task-Focused
"OK — this one has 4.8 stars, available tomorrow, 15 min away. Done." Tomás is satisficing, not optimising. First credible option that meets his threshold wins. He's not comparing.
😴 Waiting → Anxious
He goes to bed. The window before 8am is everything — morning is when the decision to cancel or continue gets made. A fast confirmation email/SMS is the highest-leverage action Opencare can take here.
😌 Relief → Gratitude
"Thank god that's over." High emotional gratitude that fades fast. The post-visit window is extremely short — any retention action must happen within 24–48 hours of treatment.
⚠️
Pain Points
No Opencare presence until Tomás is in acute pain — no preventative re-engagement channel exists for him
Emergency funnel must be fast — any multi-step friction here causes immediate bounce
Availability is invisible until NPR is submitted — Tomás can't see open slots without contacting the practice
No live availability calendar — "Available this week" is a badge, not a confirmed slot. Creates expectation mismatch
Tomás will not compare options — if the first result looks off, he exits to Google rather than scrolling
Response speed is everything — 54% of requests come after hours. If the practice doesn't respond by 8am, Tomás is already calling someone else
Patient app post-NPR state gives no urgency signal to the practice
Retention gap — gratitude is high but fades fast. No proactive path from emergency visit to regular patient relationship
Generic post-visit email doesn't capitalise on Tomás's peak gratitude moment

Oppor­tunities
✅ Quick win

Surface Opencare in "emergency dentist near me" paid and organic results. The entry point for Tomás is a crisis — Opencare must be visible exactly at that moment.

🔥 High impact

Streamline the emergency funnel to 3 steps or fewer: location → emergency type → results. Every extra question is a drop-off point for Tomás. Speed is the UX principle.

🔥 High impact

Show real-time or near-real-time availability on results cards. "Next available: Tomorrow 9am" is the single most valuable data point for Tomás — make it the primary info hierarchy element.

🔥 High impact

Implement an automated after-hours NPR alert to practices: "Emergency request received — confirm by 8am to secure booking." Puts urgency on the practice side to respond before Tomás wakes up.

🔥 High impact

Within 24h post-visit: "Emergency handled ✓ Want to make [Practice] your regular dentist?" One-tap to schedule a cleaning. Converts the emergency entry point into a long-term retention relationship.
